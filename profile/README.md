## 🚀 Overview
Harmor for Windows is an additive and subtractive synthesizer plugin developed by Image-Line, the makers of FL Studio. Instead of relying only on traditional oscillators, Harmor generates sound from a bank of partials that you can sculpt directly, then run through powerful filters and modulation. Because everything is additive under the hood, Harmor can resynthesize audio and even turn images into sound, giving Windows producers an unusually open canvas for original tones, evolving pads, and complex textures.

## ✨ Features
- **Additive synthesis engine** — Build sound from individual partials rather than fixed waveforms, giving precise control over harmonic content.
- **Audio and image resynthesis** — Import a sample or an image and let Harmor reconstruct it as fully editable additive data for creative transformation.
- **Advanced filtering** — Apply expressive filters that operate directly on partials, enabling smooth, phase-accurate sweeps that classic subtractive filters cannot match.
- **Unison and voicing** — Stack rich unison layers for wide, thick leads and pads while keeping full control over spread and phasing.
- **Flexible sound design** — Combine two independent parts, extensive modulation, and effects for deep, layered patch creation.

## 🎯 Getting Started
1. Open FL Studio or another compatible host on your Windows PC.
2. Insert Harmor on a channel or track as a VST instrument.
3. Browse the built-in presets to hear what the additive engine can do.
4. Tweak the partials, filters, and unison controls to shape your own tone.
5. Drag in a sample or image and try resynthesis to hear your source material transformed.

## ❓ FAQ
**Is Harmor free?**
Harmor is a paid instrument from Image-Line. It comes bundled with certain FL Studio editions and is also available as a paid add-on. You can try it by using the FL Studio trial. There is no free standalone full copy, so any "harmor vst free download" you may search for should be understood in that context.

**Which versions of Windows are supported?**
Harmor runs on modern 64-bit versions of Windows, including Windows 10 and Windows 11, inside FL Studio or another compatible host.

**Is my data secure?**
Harmor runs locally on your Windows machine as a plugin. Your projects, presets, and audio stay on your own system unless you choose to share them.
